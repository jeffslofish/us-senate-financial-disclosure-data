Comments

As to Part 1: The four honoraria listed stem from the same event, but are listed individually because contributions were made to four separate non-profit organizations. The four charitable organizations that received the contributions are Girls Incorporated of Delaware, University of Delaware Engineers Without Borders, First State First Lego League, and University of Delaware Borel Global Fellows. As to Part 5: The nature of this gift is a family trip with Steve and Theresa Esmond and their sons, who have been close family friends for more than 10 years. In 2016, during a family trip together to St. John, the Esmonds suffered a severe chemical poisoning at their rental property. The poisoning left Steve and their two sons in comas, followed by months spent in hospitals and rehabilitation centers recovering. Today, Steve and their sons continue to require significant medical care, including full-time nursing care, and rely on wheelchairs. As a result of their medical issues, the Esmonds cannot travel on commercial air. The Esmonds invited my family, including me, my spouse, and my three children, to join them for their first family vacation since the incident. We accompanied the Esmonds for the private chartered flight, which cost $56,000, and we stayed at a rental condo on the same property as the Esmonds, which the Esmonds paid for with points, but which had an estimated cash value of $5,000. While there were 11 people on board (the Esmonds, my family, and medical staff), I have listed the entire cost of the plane rental as a gift as required by law since I was the only Member of Congress on the flight. The Esmonds are not lobbyists and do not have any business before the Senate. The trip was entirely personal in nature. I requested pre-approval from the Ethics Committee in November 2017, and, on December 14, 2017, I received a letter advising me that the gift was permissible under the Senate Rules. (I recused myself from the Committee's work on my request, and Senator Brian Schatz served as acting Vice Chair on this matter.) While the dates of this trip cross into 2018, I am reporting the entire gift on my annual financial disclosure report for 2017, as the bulk of the trip occurred in that calendar year.
